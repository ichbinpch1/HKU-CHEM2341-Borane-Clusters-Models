Learn Theory

Wade’s rules relate cluster structure to skeletal electron pairs. For borane clusters, the key idea is to count the electrons used in cage bonding and compare that value with the expected pattern for closo, nido, and arachno structures.

Wade’s rules

closo: n + 1 • nido: n + 2 • arachno: n + 3

Here, n is the number of skeletal boron vertices in the cluster, and SEP means skeletal electron pairs. Closo clusters are closed deltahedra, nido clusters are derived by removing one vertex from the parent closo cage, and arachno clusters by removing two.

Skeletal bonding

SEP = skeletal e− / 2

In borane chemistry, not all valence electrons belong to the cluster framework. Terminal B–H bonds are exohedral and are not counted as skeletal. The skeletal count refers only to the electrons participating in cage bonding between boron atoms and in delocalized cluster bonding.

Bridging H and 3c–2e bonds

electron-deficient bonding

Many boranes contain bridging hydrogens and three-center two-electron (3c–2e) bonds. These are a hallmark of electron-deficient compounds and explain why simple localized two-center bond pictures do not fully describe borane structures.
